About Me

Hello, I am Caspian Liang (梁程炬), a Transportation PhD candidate at Beijing Jiaotong University. My work lies at the intersection of operations research, logistics systems, and AI Agent applications. I am interested in how real-world logistics constraints can be converted into transparent models, reliable optimization workflows, and verifiable decision-support prototypes.

Research and Application Interests

My current research and project experience can be summarized as a practical loop: logistics scenario understanding → optimization modeling → algorithmic validation → agent workflow design. I keep this homepage intentionally conservative: accepted publications, public-safe project summaries, and desensitized portfolio links are shown here; private materials are not published by default.

  • Operations research for logistics. Vehicle routing, electric logistics fleets, dynamic scheduling, robust optimization, and risk-aware recoverable planning.
  • AI Agent applications. Task decomposition, state tracking, tool-use workflow, logistics business rules, candidate explanation, and result validation.
  • Public-sector and enterprise logistics studies. Logistics-base evaluation, airport bonded-zone strategy, and transportation planning research with field investigation experience.

Selected Projects

  • Freight Matching Agent. A public-safe case study of an Agentic AI prototype for continuous freight-searching scenarios. The project emphasizes task decomposition, state tracking, tool-use workflow, candidate explanation, and result validation. Evidence: Top 24 / 963, finalist award. [project]

  • Modular Autonomous Logistics Fleet Routing and Robust Scheduling. A PhD research project on modular electric logistics fleets under dynamic orders, energy constraints, time windows, and demand uncertainty.
